## The question we are answering

> Which career path maximizes **(pay × long-term durability × stability)** for a mid-level 22-year-old full-stack engineer in Salzburg, Austria, with 4 years of experience, fluent in English and professional-level German, who is also the solo builder of Elyt (an agentic browser-automation platform, production, commercial traction unverified)?

## Constraints the answer must respect

1. **Income continuity.** Candidate is currently employed (Axess AG). A 6-month unpaid job-search is not acceptable. Any pivot must preserve cashflow.
2. **Austrian labor reality.** 6-week notice period, KV-IT floors (ST1 €62.6K / ST2 €76.2K), 13./14. Monatsgehalt, Abfertigung Neu. Salzburg is a tertiary tech market (~31 total software-dev postings at any time).
3. **Visa.** RWR+ held — no sponsorship needed anywhere in EU. UK/US/CA/AU remote-only or via contractor setup.
4. **Geography.** Salzburg on-site/hybrid OK. Vienna hybrid acceptable only for strong role. Remote Austria/EU/UK/US/CA/AU acceptable. No on-site relocation outside DACH.
5. **AI-replacement is a first-class criterion.** User has explicitly prioritized "my job should not get replaced by AI." Any path must be evaluated for durability against coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Devin) through at least 2031.

## Premise (user's own reframing, accepted)

**Programming languages and web frameworks are fungible with AI assist.** A 4-YOE engineer using Claude Code / Cursor can reach shipping-level fluency in a new language (Java, Go, Python, Rust, etc.) in 4–8 weeks, and pass most hiring filters as "4 YOE with [new stack]" because engineering fundamentals (architecture, debugging, delivery) transfer intact. Syntax is not the bottleneck.

**Therefore:** the strategic question is NOT "which language?" — it is **"which niche × domain × role-level × geography bundle is the highest-EV target?"**. Language is chosen last to match the chosen bundle.

## What a good answer looks like

A good answer must:

1. **Enumerate all realistic options** (not just the obvious ones). See [options.md](options.md).
2. **Evaluate each option consistently** against the same criteria. See [final.md](final.md). Specifically per option:
   - Pros / Cons
   - AT market (volume, comp distribution, employers)
   - EU market (DE/CH/NL/Nordics, remote-EU)
   - US market (remote accessibility, comp reality after AT tax)
   - Long-term outlook 2026–2031
   - AI-replacement risk with reasoning
   - Ease of pursuit for this specific candidate with reasoning
3. **Rank options** with confidence intervals, not hand-waving.
4. **Identify the single primary path + a hedge**, with decision triggers (if X happens, switch to Y).
5. **Output a 12-month action plan** concrete enough to execute without further research.

## What a good answer is NOT

- A single-bullet "pivot to AI" or "stay .NET" slogan.
- A list of trendy technologies.
- Advice that ignores Austrian labor-market specifics.
- Advice that assumes unlimited optionality (e.g. "just relocate to SF") or zero optionality (e.g. "you're stuck in Salzburg forever").
- Advice that treats Elyt as either a unicorn or invisible — it must be evaluated honestly as a portfolio artifact with unverified traction.

## Success metric

12 months from today, the candidate has:
- A signed offer at gross **≥ €85K** in AT, or **≥ €100K** remote-DACH/EU, or **≥ €120K** remote-US contractor (net-adjusted for AT tax), AND
- A role whose core work is reasonably durable against coding-agent automation through at least 2028, AND
- A skill profile that remains optionable (no dead-end lock-in) for the 2028–2031 horizon.
