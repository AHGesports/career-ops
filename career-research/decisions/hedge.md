# Hedge decision — Option F (Security / DevSecOps) at 15% effort

See: [primary.md](primary.md) · [triggers.md](triggers.md) · [../options/F-security.md](../options/F-security.md)

---

## Decision

While executing primary Option M, run a low-cost hedge into Option F (Security / DevSecOps). Expected effort: ~15% of career-development time.

## Why hedge, not fully commit

- F is the **single most AI-resistant** option on the board (see [../options/F-security.md](../options/F-security.md)).
- F has the **slowest ramp** (12–18 months, OSCP cert-gated).
- F has **no Elyt leverage** — cold pivot.
- Committing fully to F now sacrifices M's upside and Elyt's time-sensitive window.

→ Hedge = take the cheap baseline investment now so the trigger-switch to F in 2027 is faster if needed.

## Hedge actions (low-cost, high-optionality)

### Q2 2026
1. **Security+ cert.** ~€400. 3 weekends prep. Low effort, meaningful baseline credential.
2. **Subscribe to security newsletters.** Risky Business, tl;dr sec, Below Gotham Labs. Passive learning.
3. **Tag a 30-minute review of NIS2 AT implementation** to understand which employers are in a hiring forced-function.

### Q3-Q4 2026
1. **Complete 1 TryHackMe / HackTheBox challenge per week.** Maintains security muscle memory; builds habit.
2. **Read NIST SSDF / OWASP Top 10** at leisure pace. Threat-modeling familiarity.

### Cost budget
- Security+ exam: ~€400
- Study materials: ~€100 (ebook + practice)
- TryHackMe subscription: ~€15/month
- **Total: under €700 for 12 months of hedge optionality**

## When the hedge converts to primary

See [triggers.md](triggers.md). Summary:

- **Month 9 — no offer ≥ €85K on M.** Pivot remaining 6 months primarily to F.
- **Action on trigger:** start OSCP prep (~€1549 exam), target NIS2 / DORA compliance-engineering roles at Erste / Raiffeisen / OMV for spring 2027.

## What the hedge does NOT cover

- OSCP itself (expensive, high-effort — only if trigger fires).
- Offensive security specialization (red team, pen-test) — only relevant if candidate enjoys that work.
- CISSP (requires 5 YOE in security — not accessible until 2031 minimum).

## Complementary low-effort hedges (not F-specific)

Consider alongside the F hedge:
- **Network-warm at 1 Austrian security team.** 1 coffee chat with someone at Erste Security / A1 CISO office / Raiffeisen SOC. Zero cost, optional social investment.
- **Follow DORA / NIS2 enforcement news.** 10 minutes per week.

## Why not other options as hedge

- **H (distributed systems)**: even slower moat than F.
- **I (embedded)**: too slow, wrong shape.
- **J (fintech)**: already accessed through M at a fintech employer.
- **K (founding eng)**: already treated as sidequest.

F is the correct hedge because its durability advantage is maximum and its trigger-to-primary conversion path is concrete (OSCP → NIS2 role).
